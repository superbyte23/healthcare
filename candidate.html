What is EB-3 Visa?

The Employment-Based Immigration: Third Preference EB-3 visa is granted to qualified foreign workers whose skills are on the shortage designation and U.S. employer have proven no U.S. employees are available for the job.

Foreign workers under the EB-3 visa can come to the U.S. on an immigrant visa, live permanently and work without having to obtain an Employment Authorization Document (EAD). After fulfilling their employment agreement, worker may leave the sponsoring employer, look for another job, and relocate to any State they wish without fear of “buy-out,” or additional contractual obligation.

If the I-140 petition (Immigrant Petition for Foreign Worker) is approved, the spouse and unmarried children under the age of 21 may also be eligible to apply for admission to the United States.

EB-3 Visa Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for EB-3 Immigrant Visa Preference category:

  • You must be able to demonstrate that you earned a U.S. bachelor’s degree (or its foreign equivalent degree) related to the occupation, and that this degree is the normal requirement for entry into the occupation. Education and experience cannot be substituted for a bachelor’s degree;
  • You must be performing work for which qualified workers are not available in the United States
  • A Labor Certification and a permanent, full-time job offer required

Services

For new immigrants who does not have relatives or friends to help them during the transition (in addition to the new hire services employer may provide), we extend the following support:

  • Pre-Arrival
    • Find suitable temporary accommodation near public transport
    • Study local transportation system and provide schedules
  • Arrival
    • Airport meet & greet
    • Arrange airport transportation and drive to destination
    • Drive to employer location on the first day
    • Conduct cultural and community orientation
  • Guidance and Protection
    • Gateway retains the services of legal practitioners to help protect the interest of the new immigrants during their transition in the United States.
    • Conduct periodic consultation to make sure working conditions and benefits outlined in the employment contract is adhered to, and they are not exploited and discriminated in their new environment.
